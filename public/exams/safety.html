SAFETY — 산업안전지도사 (Industrial Safety Consultant)

Exam guide · Updated 2026

What it is

The Industrial Safety Consultant (산업안전지도사) is a senior-tier Korean license under the Occupational Safety and Health Act. License-holders can be retained by employers to evaluate workplace safety systems, design risk-management programs, and represent the employer in safety-related administrative proceedings. The credential sits above the more common Industrial Safety Engineer (산업안전기사) and is one of the four "consultant" tiers in Korea's workplace-safety hierarchy (alongside Health, Construction Safety, and Electrical Safety).

Eligibility

The exam is open to anyone, but practical eligibility is shaped by Stage 3 (interview), which assesses subject-matter depth that almost requires several years of safety-management work. Most successful candidates hold a related engineering degree and an Industrial Safety Engineer license already.

Exam format — three stages

  • Stage 1 — objective: common subjects (industrial safety law, industrial hygiene, ergonomics, system safety) plus one selected specialty (machine, electrical, chemical, or construction safety). 100 questions, 150 minutes.
  • Stage 2 — written essay: three subjects from the specialty area. Each requires structured analysis of a workplace-safety fact pattern. 5–6 pages per response, 120 minutes total.
  • Stage 3 — interview: a panel evaluates communication, judgment, and depth of practical safety knowledge.

Pass rate

  • Stage 1: ~30% (varies 20–40% by specialty area)
  • Stage 2: ~9–14% of Stage 1 passers
  • Stage 3: ~90% of Stage 2 passers — Stage 3 mostly weeds out candidates who clearly memorized rather than understood.

Recommended preparation

For working safety engineers, a 12-month plan is realistic:

  • Months 1–3: survey all common subjects and identify your weakest area. Build outlines.
  • Months 4–7: Stage 1 past-question drill plus targeted readings for weak topics.
  • Months 8–10: Stage 2 essay practice with structured templates per question pattern (hazard analysis, control hierarchy, incident response, management-system audit).
  • Months 11–12: Stage 3 prep — practice clear verbal explanation of typical fact patterns; record yourself and review.

Cost

Registration fee is approximately KRW 60,000 for Stage 1 and KRW 90,000 for Stage 2. Optional academy programs range from KRW 2 million for self-study packages to KRW 10+ million for live-class full-cycle programs.
