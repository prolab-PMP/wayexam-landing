HEALTH — 산업보건지도사 (Industrial Health Consultant)

Exam guide · Updated 2026

What it is

The Industrial Health Consultant (산업보건지도사) is the occupational-health counterpart to the Industrial Safety Consultant. Both fall under the same Occupational Safety and Health Act and follow a parallel three-stage exam structure, but the Health Consultant focuses on worker health rather than equipment and process safety: industrial hygiene, occupational disease, ergonomics, ventilation, exposure assessment, and health-management systems.

Eligibility

Open to anyone, with the same caveat as the Safety Consultant — the Stage 3 interview rewards real working depth. Many successful candidates have backgrounds in nursing, public health, industrial hygiene, ergonomics, or occupational medicine.

Exam format — three stages

  • Stage 1: objective. Common subjects (health law, hygiene, ergonomics, occupational disease) plus one specialty (industrial hygiene engineering, occupational medicine, or ergonomics consulting).
  • Stage 2: written essay in the specialty.
  • Stage 3: interview.

Pass rate

  • Stage 1: ~25–32% depending on the year and specialty area
  • Stage 2: ~8–13% of Stage 1 passers
  • Stage 3: ~88% of Stage 2 passers

Specialty pick

The three specialties draw different candidate pools:

  • Industrial hygiene engineering attracts engineers from chemical, semiconductor, and heavy-industry backgrounds who manage exposure controls and ventilation.
  • Occupational medicine is dominated by physicians who want a formal credential to expand into corporate health-management consulting.
  • Ergonomics consulting draws from a wider mix — public-health graduates, physical therapists, and engineers who specialize in MSD prevention.

Recommended preparation

A 10–14 month plan is realistic for full-time professionals studying part-time:

  • Months 1–3: review the common law and hygiene textbooks; sketch outlines for high-frequency essay topics.
  • Months 4–7: past-question drill for Stage 1, targeting 70%+ on each common subject before moving on.
  • Months 8–10: Stage 2 writing practice with feedback. Build a personal library of 15–20 essay templates covering frequent fact patterns (exposure-limit exceedance, MSD risk assessment, ventilation redesign, ergonomic intervention).
  • Months 11–12+: Stage 3 interview prep.

Cost

Registration fees mirror the Safety Consultant exam — approximately KRW 60,000 for Stage 1 and KRW 90,000 for Stage 2.
