CPLA — 공인노무사 (Certified Public Labor Attorney)

Exam guide · Updated 2026

What it is

The Certified Public Labor Attorney (공인노무사, often abbreviated as CPLA) is a Korean professional license, administered by the Ministry of Employment and Labor through HRD Korea. License-holders are legally authorized to represent clients in labor-related administrative procedures — collective-bargaining negotiations, unfair-dismissal disputes, workplace-safety investigations, and social-insurance filings. About 5,000 active CPLAs operate in Korea, mostly in private practice or in-house at large employers.

Eligibility

The CPLA exam is open to anyone aged 20 or older with no specific educational requirement. There is no work-experience prerequisite either. Practically, the typical successful candidate has either a law-school background, several years of HR/labor experience, or both — but the formal door is open to anyone who passes both stages.

Exam format — two stages

The CPLA exam runs in two stages, both held annually:

  • Stage 1 (1차) — objective multiple choice: five subjects (labor law I, labor law II, civil law, social insurance law, economics or business administration). 200 questions, 200 minutes. You must average 60% across all subjects and score at least 40% on every individual subject.
  • Stage 2 (2차) — written essay: three subjects (labor law I & II, HR management, plus one elective). Each question requires a 4–6 page handwritten essay analyzing a fact pattern. You must average 60% and avoid sub-40% scores.

Stage 1 passers who fail Stage 2 keep their Stage 1 pass for two more years, meaning they can take Stage 2 up to three times without retaking Stage 1.

Pass rate

Pass rates over the last decade have averaged roughly:

  • Stage 1: ~28% (year-to-year range 22–35%)
  • Stage 2: ~9% of Stage 1 passers (range 7–13%)
  • Overall successful candidates per year: about 300–400

Recommended preparation

Most successful candidates report 18–24 months of full-time-equivalent study. A typical timeline:

  • Months 1–6: work through a comprehensive labor-law textbook plus statute readings. Build the foundation of cases and key articles before any question practice.
  • Months 7–12: first Stage-1 prep cycle — past-exam questions, weekly mock tests, error-log review.
  • Months 13–18: Stage-2 essay practice. This is the bottleneck most candidates underestimate: writing a structured 5-page legal analysis under time pressure is a skill that only develops through repetition with feedback.
  • Months 19–24: integration, mock-exam-heavy phase, and targeted weakness review.

Cost

Exam-registration fees are modest (under KRW 50,000 per stage). Most candidates pay much more for prep materials and academies — a typical full-cycle CPLA prep program runs KRW 5–15 million.
