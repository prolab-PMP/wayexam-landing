Korean Pro-Exam Fees and Pass Rates Side-by-Side

2026 · ~1,400 words · 7 min read

Every prep academy publishes pass-rate statistics. Most of those statistics are misleading not because the underlying numbers are wrong, but because the comparisons are framed in ways that obscure how hard each exam actually is. This article puts the registration fees, prep-academy spend, and multi-year pass-rate data for the four credentials covered on wayexam.com (PMP, CPLA, Industrial Safety Consultant, Industrial Health Consultant) on the same page so you can see them at the same time.

Pass rates — what the brochures don't show

Pass rates are reported in three different framings, and the framing matters enormously.

Per-stage pass rate

This is the number you see most often: "Stage 1 pass rate ~28%." It tells you what fraction of people who sat that stage passed it. It's a useful internal benchmark for the exam difficulty at that stage.

Cohort pass rate (one sitting)

What fraction of people who sat Stage 1 in year N ultimately passed the entire exam that same year. For multi-stage exams this is much lower than per-stage rates because you have to clear each stage to count.

Cumulative pass rate

What fraction of people who first sat Stage 1 in year N ultimately passed the entire exam within three years (using carry-over rules). This is the most honest number for prospective candidates because it captures the multi-year reality.

What the cumulative rates mean

The 6–7% cumulative pass rate on the three Korean consultant/lawyer exams means that of every 100 people who start the journey by sitting Stage 1 for the first time, about 6 or 7 hold the license three years later. The other 93 either failed somewhere along the way and stopped, ran out of carry-over and chose not to restart, or are still in the cycle and not yet done.

That math says two things at once:

  • The exams are genuinely hard. Pass rates this low don't happen by accident.
  • The exams are mostly won by persistence. Most of the 93 who didn't pass didn't fail because they were incapable — they failed because they stopped. If you can credibly commit to the multi-year timeline, your personal expected pass rate is much higher than the cohort average.

The biggest single driver of academy spend on the three Korean exams is Stage-2 essay coaching. Stage 1 can be self-studied with public past-question banks (which is what wayexam.com provides for free); Stage 2 essay practice with mentor feedback is what people end up paying premium rates for.

Time × money trade-off

Roughly:

  • PMP: 2–3 months × ~KRW 1M total. Lowest commitment by far.
  • Safety / Health Consultant: 10–14 months × KRW 3–8M typical.
  • CPLA: 18–24 months × KRW 5–15M typical.

If you read the trade-off in reverse — "what am I buying with the time and money?" — the three Korean licenses buy regulated practice rights and a long-tail income stream as a solo or small-firm operator. PMP buys resume signal and hiring-funnel position inside corporate employment. Both can be the right answer; they're different products.
